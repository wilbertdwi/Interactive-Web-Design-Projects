History of Nintendo Consoles

Introduction

Nintendo was founded by Fusajiro Yamauchi in 1889. In the early years, Nintendo began selling Japanese playing cards. It was not until 1978 where they decided to develop video games console. In 1982, they established in Seattle, Washington and the next year, they developed their first video game console NES in the West. Their first launch was a massive success which leads to further developments of video game consoles.

Ever since then, Nintendo became known for creating both video games and games consoles. They created more classic/icon consoles such as Game Boy, Nintendo 64, DS, etc. They also introduced many players to the world of Zelda, Mario, Pokemon, etc. As a result of their massive success and popularity, they became one of the biggest video game companies in the world. This website will be looking about the history of Nintendo video consoles back from the early 80s to present day.
